About Me

I am a dedicated observational astrophysicist with a passion for transient science, exoplanet detection, and astronomical variability studies. With a strong background in instrumentation, data analysis, and Python programming, I have actively contributed to projects that bridge observational astronomy and computational techniques.

My current work spans several platforms and initiatives. I am a member of the AAVSO, where I contribute variability studies of celestial objects and report observations. I actively participate in Slooh Telescope imaging, capturing stunning celestial images like Eta Carina and De Marian. My interest in transient phenomena has led me to join the Lasair platform, where I explore real-time transient alerts and apply machine learning to alert classification. I am also a part of the Astronomy Society Glasgow (ASG), conducting structured observational studies of binary stars and recurrent novae.

My academic pursuits focus on exploring variability mechanisms in binary star systems and applying advanced statistical techniques to astrophysical data. I am particularly drawn to leveraging machine learning to enhance transit detection methods for exoplanets, refining light curve analysis, and extending these methods to large-scale surveys like LSST and ZTF.
