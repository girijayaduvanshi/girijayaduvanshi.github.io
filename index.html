About Me

I am a passionate observational astrophysicist with advanced degrees in physics and a strong blend of technical skills in instrumentation, data analysis, and Python programming. I have actively contributed to multiple astrophotography, data processing, and variability studies, combining my technical expertise with a deep interest in exploring the universe.

My work with platforms like Slooh and the American Association of Variable Star Observers (AAVSO) has enhanced my skills in imaging and reporting celestial observations. Using Slooh telescopes, I’ve conducted imaging sessions and reported findings to AAVSO, focusing on variability studies of stars and exoplanet transits.

Recently, I’ve been deeply involved in the Lasair transient alert platform, exploring and analyzing transient phenomena through its staged approach and API system. This includes running customized queries, leveraging Grafana for visual insights, and working on transient classification using machine learning techniques.

My ongoing projects include anomaly detection and Bayesian analysis of Algol (Beta Persei) and TCrB (T Coronae Borealis), which involve generating high-precision light curves, emission maps, and identifying transient anomalies in binary systems and nebulae.

I am also keenly interested in:

  • Expanding my work on exoplanet detection through the transit method using tools like the EXOTIC framework.
  • Exploring computational astrophysics and the application of machine learning in astronomy.
  • Participating in projects related to upcoming surveys like LSST-TiDES and ZTF (Zwicky Transient Facility).

As an active member of the Astronomy Society Glasgow (ASG), I frequently engage in observational sessions using binoculars and a 16-inch dome telescope, enhancing my photometric analysis skills. My work aligns closely with my fascination for lunar exploration, stellar evolution, and transient astrophysics.
